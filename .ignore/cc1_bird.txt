  A rigorous 18-section derivation covering:

  1. Governing equations — Unsteady Navier-Stokes on a time-varying domain Ω(t),
   with no-slip on the moving wing surface S_w(t). Force = surface integral of
  Cauchy stress tensor.
  2. Nondimensional numbers — Reynolds (Re = ρUc/μ, ~10⁴–10⁶), Strouhal (St =
  fA/U, efficient range 0.2–0.4), reduced frequency (k = πfc/U, O(0.1–1) for
  birds → unsteady effects essential).
  3. Wing kinematics — Heaving h(r,t) = H(r)sin(ωt) and pitching α(r,t) = α₀ +
  Θ·sin(ωt+ϕ). Effective AoA: α_eff ≈ α − ḣ/U. Key insight: a flapping wing
  continuously changes the aerodynamic angle of attack.
  4. Quasi-steady lift — L′ = ½ρU_eff²c(r)C_L(α_eff); thin-airfoil: C_L ≈
  2πα_eff. Time-averaged lift must equal mg.
  5. Thrust generation — Aerodynamic force tilts forward by angle β ≈ ḣ/U during
   downstroke. T′ ≈ F′·(ḣ/U).
  6. Momentum theory — mg ~ ρSUw; induced power P_i ~ (mg)^(3/2) / √(2ρS).
  Explains why large wing area reduces energy cost.
  7. Vorticity formulation — L′ = ρUΓ (Kutta-Joukowski); Kelvin's theorem means
  changing bound circulation sheds vortex loops. Force ≈ −dI/dt where I is
  vortex impulse.
  8. Theodorsen model — Unsteady lift = added-mass term (accelerates surrounding
   air) + circulatory term (C(k) Theodorsen function). Linear model incomplete
  at high AoA.
  9. Added mass — F_a′ ~ ρc²ω²H. Why high-frequency flapping is expensive.
  10. Wingbeat power — P_muscle = P_induced + P_profile + P_parasite +
  P_inertial. Classic U-shaped power curve: P(U) ≈ A/U + BU³ + C.
  11. Downstroke dominance — Wing extended at max area; AoA tuned for positive
  lift+thrust. Upstroke: wing folded, feathers separate (slotted flow) to reduce
   drag.
  12. Leading-edge vortices (LEV) — At high AoA, LEV creates low-pressure
  suction lift exceeding static stall limit. Stabilized by spanwise flow, twist,
   sweep, feather porosity.
  13. Spanwise structure — V(r,t) ≈ U + φ̇r; outer wing does most aerodynamic
  work. Torque ~ ∫r·dF(r,t) — distal force is costly.
  14. Wing twist — Required to keep α_eff near optimum across the span: θ(r,t) ≈
   α* + atan(V_z/V_x). Wings are aeroelastic structures.
  15. Aeroelasticity — ρ_s A y_tt + EIy_ssss = f_aero + f_muscle. Cauchy number
  Ca = ρU²L³/EI governs compliance.
  16. Wake topology — Vortex rings of impulse I ~ ρΓπR²; force ~ f·I. Rings
  tilted for thrust.
  17. Minimal model — 7-equation reduced-order model capturing: kinematics →
  α_eff → Γ(t) → vortex wake → lift/thrust/power.
  18. Deepest picture — "Birds fly by imposing time-dependent circulation on a
  deforming finite wing, thereby shedding a structured vortex wake whose impulse
   equals the aerodynamic force." Evolution minimizes P̄ subject to ⟨Fz⟩=mg,
  ⟨Fx⟩=D, stall avoidance, and structural constraints.
